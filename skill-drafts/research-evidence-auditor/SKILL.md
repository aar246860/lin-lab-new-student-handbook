# Research Evidence Auditor

Draft skill for source-to-claim checking.

## When to Use

Use before:

- Submitting a report.
- Sending a manuscript to coauthors.
- Updating a public webpage.
- Accepting AI-generated text.
- Turning OCR or notes into formal content.

## Audit Steps

1. List each claim.
2. Identify its evidence source.
3. Classify it as source-backed, simplified, schematic, synthetic, inferred, or unsupported.
4. Flag missing data, missing code, missing citation, and overstatement.
5. Rewrite unsupported claims conservatively.

## Evidence Boundary

The auditor does not decide scientific novelty by itself. It checks whether the current evidence supports the current wording.

## Do Not

- Treat AI output as evidence.
- Convert a schematic into a field result.
- Hide uncertainty by polishing prose.
- Expose private OCR, student data, or unpublished collaborator data.
