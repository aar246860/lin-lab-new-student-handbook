# Lin Lab Onboarding Map

Draft skill for turning a student's entry point into a concrete training path.

## When to Use

Use for:

- New student orientation.
- Summer training plans.
- Weekly task sequencing.
- Research readiness self-assessment.
- Converting handbook rules into a checklist.

## Output

Return:

1. Student starting assumptions.
2. 4-8 week training route.
3. Required weekly deliverables.
4. Evidence to check.
5. Advisor-facing risk notes.

## Evidence Boundary

Use only the handbook, current project files, and user-provided student context. Do not infer personality, motivation, or ability from sparse notes.

## Do Not

- Invent lab rules not in the handbook.
- Rank students.
- Turn onboarding into a punitive checklist.
- Omit reproducibility, weekly notes, and source tracking.
