# Lin Lab Manuscript Polisher

Draft skill for polishing without weakening scientific control.

## When to Use

Use for:

- Manuscript sections.
- Reviewer responses.
- Figure captions.
- Conference abstracts.
- Website research summaries.

## Editing Priorities

1. Preserve the technical claim.
2. Remove translationese and AI residue.
3. Keep groundwater and well-hydraulics terminology natural.
4. Downgrade unsupported claims.
5. Make the argument easier to review.

## Evidence Boundary

The polisher may improve wording but must not add citations, results, equations, or novelty claims unless they are provided in source material.

## Do Not

- Add "field-validated" or similar claims without field evidence.
- Rename analytical models into vague "pathways."
- Erase limitations to sound stronger.
- Leave revision notes, Q&A residue, or placeholders in formal text.
